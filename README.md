### 
Climate change is a phenomenon that occurs on multiple time scales, often affecting human activities; while part of climate
change is dominated by physically processes such as weather, El Nino, the North Atlantic Oscillation, Asian Monsoon
variations, droughts, and circulations of the atmosphere and oceans, a significant part of climate change is also due to
anthropogenic change, resulting in global warming, ozone holes, deforestation, pollution and other ecosystem changes.
Thus, as a result of climate change, rising temperatures, shifting precipitation patterns, and frequent weather events are
threatening the stability and productivity of agricultural systems worldwide, leading to decreased crop yields and food
security and disruptions in water availability.
Furthermore, research from the Environmental Protection Agency and Oxfam emphasizes how the unpredictability of
climate conditions are making it harder for farmers, especially in developing countries where agriculture is a primary source
of food, to manage crop production effectively and therefore exacerbating global hunger due to heightened risks of crop
failures from intense climate change phenomenon. A study by NASA further warns of significant declining yields in major
crops such as corn and wheat, highlighting the consequences for global food supply chains and the urgency for farmers
and policymakers to strategize and adapt to different agricultural practices as a result of climate change. Moreover, The
Economist adds how climate change is expected to shift agricultural zones – for example, crops such as coffee and grape
face reduced yields in traditional growing areas while new regions such as northern Europe could be more prone to harvest
crops such as maize. Consequently, the article points to global trade as a measure to accommodate for the geographic shift
in agriculture due to various climate change phenomena.
Through data visualizations such as a geographical analysis, clustering, and predictive models, we can illustrate the
impacts of climate change on agriculture, allowing analysis on complex patterns and trends across various geographic
regions across the globe. Through further visual and exploratory analysis, we can highlight the relationships between
different climate change variables and agricultural outcomes.
By using machine learning model, we want to also effetively predict the change of crop production, which allows policy
makers enact preventional stratagies.
